Michael Franke Fort Wayne Komet President &amp; Co-owner died

In a tragic development, Michael Franke, the President and Co-owner of Fort Wayne Komets, passed away on December 10 at the Stillwater Hospice in Fort Wayne. His death comes after a lengthy battle with cancer.

Michael Franke: A Pillar of the Fort Wayne Komets

Being the longtime team president and co-owner of the Komets, Franke was instrumental in steering the team’s fortunes. The 63-year-old’s dedication to the sport and his team was evident in his lifelong commitment to the Komets.

The Man Behind the Success

Franke’s leadership and passion for hockey were key elements of the Komets’ success. His vision and hard work helped shape the team into one of the most respected franchises in the ECHL, making his loss even more profound.

Remembering Michael Franke

Tributes have been pouring in from all corners of the hockey community, underlining the impact Franke had on the sport and his team. His legacy will live on through the Fort Wayne Komets and the countless lives he touched throughout his illustrious career.

A Battle Bravely Fought

Despite his long battle with cancer, Franke remained dedicated to his team until the end. His resilience and determination were an inspiration to many and will continue to be remembered.

As we mourn the loss of this great leader, it’s important to celebrate his life and achievements. Franke’s contribution to the Fort Wayne Komets and the world of hockey is immeasurable. His legacy will continue to inspire future generations of athletes and sports enthusiasts.
