Ned Started a Career as a Legal Practitioner in the UK Long Before His Marriage to Regina

When Ned completed his education and was called to the bar in England, he stayed back in the country and started his legal practice career. He worked at the crown prosecution service and qualified as a solicitor of the Supreme Court of England and Wales.

The philanthropist had other careers in the legal sphere with Kumars Solicitors and Pascaldies & Co Solicitors and later founded Ned Nwoko & Co Solicitors in London.

Ned led most of his career life in the United Kingdom, especially in the 1990s. Other positions he held before his return to Nigeria include serving as the secretary general of Nigerian Legal Practitioners UK and as a visiting adviser to the Citizens’ Advice Bureau in London.

His legal career in the UK skyrocketed so fast that his firm, Ned Nwoko & Co Solicitors, became very big. Also, he was recognized as one of the best black lawyers of African descent.

Regina Daniels’ Husband Became a House of Representative Member Shortly Before He Turned 39

Upon the return of democracy in Nigeria, Ned contested for the House of Representative seat of the Aniocha/Oshimili federal constituency in 1998. He won the elections and was sworn in May 1999.

At the time, he was 38 years old, and it was only a few months to his 39th birthday. He served for a tenure from 1999 to 2003 when he was 42.

Ned Nwoko Has Been Very Active in Economic Reforms 

The businessman has been active in things that concern the Nigerian economy and financial laws. During the Olusegun Obasanjo‘s regime, he was vital in the Paris Club loan refunds.

At the time, he wrote a letter that led to the discontinuation of monthly deductions from the state’s allocation and the commencement of refunds to states. This move would lead states to recover more than $20 billion of deductions from foreign loans.

In another loan deduction saga, he stood in as a legal practitioner and helped about 774 Local Government Areas in Nigeria get a refund of $3.2 billion following the 2013 court judgment.

He Was Sworn in as a Nigerian Senator at 62

In 2022, Ned won the senatorial ticket of the People’s Democratic Party (PDP) in the Delta North senatorial district. In February 2023, he won the general elections and was sworn in as a Nigerian senator in May 2023.

At the time that the lawyer cum politician became a senator, he was 62 years old, while his famous wife Regina was 22. As a senator, Nwoko has moved several motions, including urging for the investigation of crude oil theft in the Niger Delta.

Again, towards the fall of 2023, he moved a motion, raising an alarm of the constant kidnappings in Nigeria’s capital, Abuja. Following the motion, the FCT minister Nyesom Wike was asked to revisit the Abuja closed-circuit television (CCTV) project issue.

A Look at Ned Nwoko’s Life as a Philanthropist

Ned Nwoko is a renowned philanthropist whose giving has gone beyond Nigeria’s shores. As part of his works, he founded the Prinf Ned Nwoko Foundation which has given to different causes, ranging from culture and tourism to sports, health, and education.

One of the most notable works he has done through the Non-Governmental Organization (NGO) is his participating ardently in malaria eradication project.

Through this work, his foundation has supported research and works to create an anti-malaria vaccine. In 2020, he flagged off a malaria campaign in Antarctica, leading him to become the first black African to visit the South Pole.
