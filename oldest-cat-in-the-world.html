Oldest Cat In The World

The oldest cat in the world is Creme Puff.

She enjoyed a diet of bacon, eggs, asparagus, broccoli, and coffee with heavy cream each morning.

Additionally, every other day, she savored a few drops of red wine.

This unique regimen contributed to her remarkable 38-year and 3-day lifespan.

In recent times, numerous cats have enjoyed lengthy lives, becoming beloved family members cherished by their families for many years.

These long-lived felines defy categorization, hailing from diverse backgrounds, breeds, and corners of the world.

The common thread uniting them is the love and exceptional care bestowed upon them by their devoted owners and families.

    #1. Creme Puff – 38 Years 3 Days

    Creme Puff holds the title as the oldest cat ever, living an impressive 38 years and 3 days.

    Born on August 3, 1967, she peacefully passed away on August 6, 2005, under the care of Granpa Rex Allen’s owner.

    Creme Puff’s daily routine included a hearty breakfast of bacon and eggs, asparagus, broccoli, and coffee with heavy cream.

    Every other day, she indulged in a few drops of red wine.

    Her owner’s dedication extended to creating wooden resting spots on the walls of the home, ensuring comfort for their beloved feline companions.

    #2. Baby – 38 Years

    The second-oldest cat in recorded history, Baby, was a black domestic house cat residing with Al Palusky and his mother, Mabel, in Duluth, Minnesota.

    Baby spent the first 28 years of his life within the confines of the house where he was born.

    It wasn’t until Al’s marriage that changes occurred.

    Baby’s new owner, concerned about the furniture and the cat’s claws, arranged for Baby’s first visit to a veterinarian for declawing.

    This feline was not particularly fond of children, often seeking refuge behind furniture when they were around.

    Al attributes Baby’s remarkable longevity to the exercise he got each time he used his litter box or sought food.

    Baby’s food bowl and litter box were positioned in the basement, requiring him to traverse 14 steps up and down each time he needed them.

    #3. Puss – 36 Years 1 Day

    Puss, a male tabby of mysterious origins, is said to have been born on November 28, 1903, in Devon, England.

    Remarkably, he lived until November 29, 1934, passing away just one day after celebrating his 36th birthday.

    #4. Ma – 34 Years 5 Months

    Ma, a fortunate female tabby, found her home with Alice St. George Moore in Drewsteignton, England.

    Ma’s diet primarily consisted of fresh meat from the local butcher.

    #5. Grandpa Rex Allen – 34 Years 2 Months

    Grandpa Rex Allen was adopted by Jake Perry in 1970 from the Humane Society.

    He was born on February 1, 1964, and became a cherished member of Perry’s family.

    Grandpa even achieved the title of a grandmaster from the International Cat Association, the highest honor for a household cat.

    This unique Sphynx and Devon Rex cross had an unusual liking for broccoli, often having it for breakfast.
